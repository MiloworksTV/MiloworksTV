# Milo — Series Arc & Writers' Room

> Operational reference for generating daily episodes. The `README.md`
> says *what the show is*; this file says *what happens across the
> year*. New daily manifests are derived from this file plus
> `style_bible.md` and `daily_writer.md`.

---

## The Year of Becoming — at a glance

| Quarter | Days | Earth season | Beat for Milo |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | 1–90 | Spring | **Settling in.** Milo learns the basics. Pip warms (slightly). Sky events are tiny and ignorable. The magazine is fat. |
| Q2 | 91–180 | Summer | **The Second Blob.** Around Day 90 a real comet lands. Tension. Most episodes still curriculum, but the second blob recurs. By Day 180 the second blob is either a friend, a rival, or has left. |
| Q3 | 181–270 | Autumn | **Earth gets weirder.** Halloween, the first time Milo sees fallen leaves. Pip starts school again — Milo wrestles with absence for the first time. The sky events get less ignorable. |
| Q4 | 271–365 | Winter → spring | **The Choice.** Milo has been here a year. Something from the homeworld asks him to come back. He has to decide on Day 365 whether to stay. The finale rhymes with Ep 0. |

Day 1 = the day after the user generates the first daily episode. The
canonical mapping (Day 1 = Monday) makes the weekday buckets line up
on the calendar, but the show works without it.

## Weekday curriculum buckets

The bucket assigned to each weekday never changes. The specific
concept inside the bucket is picked when the daily manifest is written.
Aim to **never repeat a concept inside the same quarter** — there are
plenty.

### Mon — *Feelings*

The hardest bucket and the heart of the show. A concept here is an
emotion or emotional state.

> Pool: patience, jealousy, boredom, embarrassment, hope, pride,
> missing someone, anger that passes, loneliness, gratitude, surprise,
> anticipation, regret, awe, contentment, frustration, relief, shame,
> tenderness, nostalgia, courage, disappointment, joy that hurts a
> little, calm, restlessness, forgiveness, stage fright, FOMO,
> homesickness, pity, second-hand embarrassment, "the feeling
> something's about to end", being moved by something small.

### Tue — *How Things Work*

Concrete physical-world phenomena. The "science" bucket — never
lectured, always experienced.

> Pool: gravity, shadows, ice melting, wind, echoes, mirrors,
> batteries, locks and keys, magnets, rust, rain, evaporation,
> reflections in puddles, static electricity, springs, ramps, gears,
> friction, heat rising, the way sound carries over water, soap
> bubbles, why metal feels colder than wood at the same temperature,
> a sponge, a magnifying glass, the way honey pours, the way paper
> cuts, knots, balance, momentum, condensation on a cold glass, the
> way a leaf falls.

### Wed — *People Rules*

Social conventions. The most fertile satire bucket — the alien is
genuinely confused and the audience over 25 also thinks these rules
are weird.

> Pool: sharing, please/thank you, taking turns, lying (white,
> kind, panicked), apologies, secrets, "inside voice", "use your
> words", waiting in line, knocking before entering, eye contact,
> personal space, hand-shakes, small talk, saying goodbye twice,
> not talking with your mouth full, holding the door, "after you",
> "I'm fine" (when not fine), the rule that you laugh at a friend's
> joke even when it's bad, gift-giving, the rule that a question
> isn't always a question, mercy, generosity, "fair", letting
> someone win on purpose, accepting a compliment, deflecting a
> compliment, when to tell on someone and when not to.

### Thu — *Words*

**Idioms only.** Pip says one. Milo takes it literally. He
fails physically.

> Pool: hold your horses, spill the beans, raining cats and dogs,
> break a leg, piece of cake, kill two birds with one stone, the
> ball is in your court, beat around the bush, bite the bullet,
> burn the midnight oil, by the skin of your teeth, costs an arm
> and a leg, get cold feet, hit the nail on the head, jump on the
> bandwagon, let the cat out of the bag, miss the boat, on cloud
> nine, once in a blue moon, pull yourself together, see eye to
> eye, the last straw, time flies, under the weather, when pigs
> fly, you can't judge a book by its cover, butterflies in your
> stomach, a frog in your throat, a chip on your shoulder, head
> over heels, a heart of gold, an open book, the apple of my eye.

### Fri — *Big Questions*

The bucket where the show earns the *Midnight Gospel* note. Quiet,
philosophical, never preachy. The morph here is usually slow and
beautiful instead of fast and funny.

> Pool: time, dreams, infinity, where do thoughts go, what is
> "tomorrow", what is "later", where do feelings go when they
> end, what is a friend, what is "real", does the moon sleep,
> where was I before I was here, do shadows have shadows, do
> trees know each other, what is a memory, where does a song
> live when nobody's singing it, why do things end, what is "the
> same", what makes today today, what's inside a question, can
> you miss something you've never had, where does the sky stop,
> what is between two seconds.

### Sat — *Pure Being* (chain-rest)

No concept. The episode is a hangout. The backyard at a different
hour: pre-dawn fog, midday rain, dusk with fireflies, a windy
afternoon. One tiny visual gag (a leaf lands on Milo and he
respectfully refuses to move; the gnome's hat blows off; a stray
cat passes through). Pip might not appear. The chain breathes.

### Sun — *Recap morph* (chain-rest)

Pip reads on the lawn. Milo, half-asleep beside her, idly
half-morphs through the week's five concepts in a single quiet
2-minute drift — the patience-stretch from Monday, the shadow from
Tuesday, the half-shared shape from Wednesday, the literal idiom
from Thursday, the slow Big Question from Friday. The week in one
breath. No new lesson. Compounds the previous week.

## Sky events calendar

Roughly monthly. Always **distant, brief, easily missed.** Pip never
sees them. Milo always does. They are the slow drumbeat under the
year.

| Day | Event | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| 12 | A single falling star, very high. | Sweet, ignorable. |
| 30 | Brief faint pink shimmer in twilight clouds. | Wistful. |
| 45 | Midday double rainbow — one arc is the wrong color (mint, not green). | Curious. |
| 60 | Three falling stars in quick succession. | First small worry. |
| 75 | A single cloud at sunset pulses gently, like it's breathing. | Eerie-beautiful. Milo pauses. |
| **90** | **A second comet streaks down and lands offscreen, somewhere in the neighborhood.** Milo's quiet payoff frame is interrupted by the sound of impact. | The arrival. |
| 120 | Faint aurora at the wrong latitude. | Ongoing tension. |
| 150 | The moon, for one frame, is slightly the wrong shape. | Surreal. |
| 180 | A falling star that doesn't fall — it hovers a moment and goes back up. | Mid-year hinge. |
| 210 | Brief eclipse where the sun looks mint-green. | Echoes Milo. |
| 240 | Tiny distant comets like fireflies along the horizon. | The homeworld is closer. |
| 270 | First real Earth snowfall (not a sky event from Milo's people, but it scares him just as much). | "The sky is broken." |
| 300 | Northern lights overhead, in pinks and greens. | Beautiful and pointed. |
| 330 | A long low ripple in the clouds, horizon to horizon. | Something is coming. |
| 360 | All week, every night: a single distant pulse, like a heartbeat. | The finale build. |
| **365** | The finale event. Reserve. Plan in Q4 once we know what the year wanted to say. | TBD. |

## Compounding form drift

**Rule:** Milo's *default* form (the teardrop he returns to between
morphs) gains exactly **one tiny permanent change every ~10
episodes.** Nothing big. Never more than one detail per drift. Never
removed once added.

The drift list is appended to as the show runs. The first ten:

| Drift | Around day | Change |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 10 | A single coral-pink freckle appears just under his eye. |
| 2 | 20 | His right antenna develops a small kink near the tip. |
| 3 | 30 | His pupil now contains one fixed star-shaped sparkle (used to drift). |
| 4 | 40 | His base glow shifts half a step warmer (mint → mint-cream). |
| 5 | 50 | A faint inner stripe of slightly darker mint runs vertically through his body. |
| 6 | 60 | A second tiny freckle appears, this one juice-box pink (callback to Ep 0). |
| 7 | 70 | His outline is now just barely thicker on his right side. |
| 8 | 80 | His default rests one pixel taller than it used to. |
| 9 | 90 | His glow gains a faint warm pulse on the heartbeat — visible only in close-ups. |
| 10 | 100 | After the second blob arrives, his outline very faintly carries a second color (whatever the second blob is). |

Subsequent drifts are decided in batches of 10 once we see how the
show is settling. The drift list lives in this file; the episode
manifest must restate the *current* drift state in its prompt so the
model doesn't reset it.

## Daily episode template

Every daily manifest follows this shape. Numbers are guideline
durations; ±2 s is fine. The total should land between **55–65 s**.
The full operational version of this scaffold (with hard rules,
self-check list, and prompt-writing guidance) lives in
[`daily_writer.md`](daily_writer.md).

```yaml
slug: day-NNN-<concept-slug>
title: "Milo — Day NNN: <Concept>"
day: NNN
weekday: <Mon|Tue|Wed|Thu|Fri|Sat|Sun>
bucket: <feelings|how-things-work|people-rules|words|big-questions|pure-being|recap>
concept: "<the human concept of the day>"
prev_episode: "<slug of the episode whose last_frame seeds shot 01>"
handoff_scene: "06"
form_drift_state: "<one-line description of Milo's current default-form variations>"
aspect_ratio: "16:9"
resolution: "720p"

music:
  track: satie-gymnopedie-1
  volume_db: -2
  fade_in_s: 1.5
  fade_out_s: 3.0

scenes:
  - id: "01"  # Cold open — pick up exactly where we left off
    mode: image
    image_from: "_prev_episode"
    duration: 10
  - id: "02"  # Concept hook — Pip points at today's topic
    mode: image
    image_from: "01"
    duration: 10
  - id: "03"  # Wrong attempt — one alien-style body morph
    mode: image
    image_from: "02"
    duration: 12
  - id: "04"  # Becomes the concept (signature sustained pose)
    mode: image
    image_from: "03"
    duration: 14
  - id: "05"  # Quiet payoff — morph settles, ❤️/💡 thought-bubble
    mode: image
    image_from: "04"
    duration: 8
  - id: "06"  # Hand-off frame — clean stable image, becomes tomorrow's seed
    mode: image
    image_from: "05"
    duration: 8
```

This is the silent + scored version: 60-second runtime, no spoken
dialogue (the show is silent — see [`daily_writer.md`](daily_writer.md)),
post-mixed with Satie's *Gymnopédie No. 1* at stitch time.

**Hand-off frame rules** (shot 06 — the most important shot in the
show):

1. The world must be in its **default state**: same backyard, same
   props, no morph in progress, no special lighting.
2. Milo must be in his **current default form** including the
   form-drift state, holding still in a clear pose.
3. Pip is either offscreen or in a clear stable pose (rare; default
   is offscreen, juice box on the patio).
4. Time of day must be explicit in the prompt — the next episode's
   shot 01 will quote it back.
5. The audio block ends in **ambient only** (no dialogue, no
   stinger). Music tail off.

**Cold-open rules** (shot 01 — every day after Day 1):

1. The prompt opens by quoting the previous day's hand-off frame
   verbatim where possible.
2. State the time elapsed: "minutes later", "the next morning",
   "later that day", "after a long pause".
3. Milo can begin a small idle morph in the first second to assert
   that the chain is alive (a half-stretch, a slow blink, an
   antenna twitch).

## Week 1 — concrete plan

| Day | Date | Bucket | Concept | One-line pitch |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Mon | Feelings | **Patience** | Pip says "wait." Milo cannot. He stretches forward through time, snaps back, learns waiting is staying still while the world keeps going. |
| 2 | Tue | How Things Work | **Shadows** | Milo befriends his shadow. The shadow is rude. He becomes his own shadow to negotiate. |
| 3 | Wed | People Rules | **Sharing** | Pip's second juice box. "Half" is unspeakable. Milo splits into two half-Milos. Pip is *not* relieved. |
| 4 | Thu | Words | **"Hold your horses"** | Four cartoon horses sprout out of Milo's body. He earnestly tries to hold them. They will not be held. |
| 5 | Fri | Big Questions | **Tomorrow** | Milo asks where "tomorrow" is kept. He looks under the kiddie pool. The episode ends on him still looking. |
| 6 | Sat | Pure Being | — | Pre-dawn. Foggy backyard. Milo finds dew on the gnome and quietly tastes it. No dialogue. |
| 7 | Sun | Recap morph | — | Pip reads on the lawn. Milo half-morphs through Mon–Fri's concepts in one slow drift. |

Day 1 is checked in at `episodes/day-001-patience/manifest.yaml` and
inherits its first frame from Ep 0's hand-off shot (Milo sitting alone
on the lawn at twilight, magazine in his lap, Pip already gone
inside). See `episodes/ep00-the-landing/manifest.yaml` for the
canonical seed and `episodes/ep00-the-landing/output/last_frame.png`
for the actual handoff PNG.

Subsequent weeks are sketched at the start of each week — pull from
the bucket pools above, avoiding any concept already used in the
current quarter.

## Author's notes

- **Self-contained first.** Any episode should land for a viewer who
  has never seen the others. The arc rewards repeat viewing; it never
  punishes drop-in viewing.
- **Pip is the audience surrogate, not the alien.** When Pip is bored
  by Milo, the audience laughs. When Pip is quietly moved by Milo,
  the audience is too. Use her sparingly — she appears in 1–3 of
  the 6 shots, not all of them.
- **Spice, not snark.** The "sarcasm" in the Pocoyo + Futurama mix
  comes from Pip's flat *facial* delivery (the show is silent — no
  voiced dialogue, ever) and from Milo earnestly missing the
  point — not from punching down. Never mock the concept itself;
  the concepts are sacred. Mock the gap between Milo and the
  concept.
- **The model is an actor with no memory.** Every prompt restates the
  world, the characters, the form-drift state, the time of day, and
  the morph state. Trust nothing to carry across calls.
- **One concept per episode.** If a daily pitch needs two concepts to
  land, it's two episodes.
